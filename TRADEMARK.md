# Trademark & Naming Policy

The source code of this project is licensed under Apache-2.0 and the documentation
under CC-BY-4.0. **Those licenses do not grant rights to use the project's name or
marks.** This is how a permissively-licensed standard stays trustworthy: anyone may
use and fork the code, but the **name remains the canonical source**.

## Marks

- **"finance-research-eval"** and **"MARBO FINANCE"** are marks of MARBO FINANCE.

## You may (nominative / descriptive use)

- State that your product **"uses"**, **"is built on"**, or **"is compatible with"**
  finance-research-eval.
- Say that your analysis **"passes the finance-research-eval gates (G-1..G-6)"**,
  provided it actually does (against an unmodified version of the gates).
- Cite the specification per `LICENSE-docs` (CC-BY-4.0) with attribution.

## You may not (without written permission)

- Use the name or marks as (or in) the name of **your own** product, service, or
  company in a way that suggests endorsement or official status.
- Use the marks on a **modified** version of the gates/standard in a way that
  implies it is the official standard. If you fork and change the gates, rename
  your variant clearly.
- Claim a result is **"finance-research-eval certified"** — there is no
  certification program (a result either passes the published gates or it doesn't).

## Why this exists

The value of the public standard is that "passes the finance-research-eval gates"
**means one specific, verifiable thing**. Trademark protection (not copyleft) is
what prevents that meaning from being diluted by modified-but-same-named variants.
